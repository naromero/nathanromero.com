Get to know me.

Who is this guy anyway?

Well, I'm sure that by now you've figured that I am Nathan Romero. I'm a 6'4", brown haired, hazel-eyed graphic designer based out of San Francisco, CA. I recently graduated from UC San Diego with a B.A. in Cognitive Science and have jumped into employment rather quickly since graduation. Despite enduring four tenuous years at the university, I have come to terms with the fact that I probably will not pursue a career in the cognitive science field. Instead, I have been fortunate enough to find a full-time career right out of college in something I am truly passionate for--graphic design.

This career decision wasn’t by any means rash because truthfully, design has been a hobby of mine since I was a freshman in high school. It wasn't until I landed a job at a design studio my junior year of college that I considered turning my hobby into a viable career choice. At this studio I was given the opportunity to design something new everyday and picked up design theory along the way. Given the amount of high-exposure projects I designed, I really began to hone my skills has a graphic designer. My designs were seen by a large student body and I regard my time at this design studio as some of the most transformative years of my life.

Tell me something interesting. Hobbies?

As far as hobbies go, the list is pretty short at the moment. I began my employment only 3 days after my last final and a lot of my free time now is spent "settling-in". Graphic design was usually what I did with my free time--after homework was done and tests were studied for, of course. Now that midterms are a thing of the past and my “9-5” is spent designing, I'm left with a lot of free time. During these hours, you can probably find me reading up on HTML/CSS, spending time with my girlfriend, or at a local card room taking some sucker’s money.
